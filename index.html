Risk Corner: Crash Impact by Asset Class

Understanding how different asset classes react during a market crash is crucial for managing risk. This chart shows hypothetical percentage impacts on different parts of the portfolio during a significant downturn (e.g., a 30% drop in overall equity markets).

During a market crash, stocks and equity-heavy mutual funds typically see the largest declines. Debt and Fixed Deposits (FDs) offer stability with minimal impact, while gold often acts as a hedge, potentially increasing in value, providing a cushion to the overall portfolio.

Tips & Notes for Your Portfolio

  • Diversification: Spreading investments across different asset classes (stocks, mutual funds, gold, debt) helps reduce overall risk. Don't put all your eggs in one basket!
  • Systematic Investment Plans (SIPs): Regular, disciplined investments (like SIPs in mutual funds) average out the cost of acquisition over time, reducing the impact of market volatility.
  • Hedges: Assets like Gold and Debt/FDs act as hedges, providing stability and potential gains when equity markets are volatile or declining.
  • Long-Term Horizon: This portfolio is designed for a 10-year horizon. Short-term market fluctuations are normal; staying invested for the long term helps ride out volatility and achieve compounding returns.
  • Rebalancing: Periodically review and adjust your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation (e.g., if stocks grow significantly, you might trim them to bring them back to 40% of the total portfolio). This helps manage risk and lock in gains.
  • Stay Informed: While this dashboard provides a snapshot, continuously educate yourself about market trends and economic indicators.
